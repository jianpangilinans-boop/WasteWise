Waste Segregation Guide

Learn how to properly segregate your waste for a cleaner environment

Why Waste Segregation Matters

Proper waste segregation is crucial for efficient recycling, reducing landfill waste, and protecting our environment. By separating your waste correctly, you contribute to a cleaner, greener Cabanatuan City.

Biodegradable Waste

Organic waste that can naturally decompose

What Goes Here:

  • Food scraps and leftovers
  • Fruit and vegetable peels
  • Garden waste (leaves, grass clippings)
  • Paper towels and napkins (soiled)
  • Coffee grounds and tea bags
  • Eggshells and nutshells

Pro Tip: Start composting at home to reduce waste and create nutrient-rich soil for your plants!

Non-Biodegradable Waste

Materials that cannot decompose naturally

What Goes Here:

  • Plastic bags and wrappers
  • Styrofoam and foam containers
  • Disposable diapers and sanitary products
  • Rubber products and leather
  • Ceramics and broken glass
  • Mixed material packaging (hard to recycle)

Important: Reduce usage of non-biodegradable items when possible. Choose reusable alternatives!

Recyclable Waste

Materials that can be processed and reused

What Goes Here:

  • Clean plastic bottles and containers
  • Paper, cardboard, and magazines
  • Aluminum cans and metal containers
  • Glass bottles and jars
  • Tetra packs and juice boxes
  • Tin cans and metal lids

Remember: Clean and dry recyclables before disposal. Remove caps and labels when possible!

Special/Hazardous Waste

Materials requiring special handling

What Goes Here:

  • Batteries (all types)
  • Electronics and appliances
  • Fluorescent bulbs and CFLs
  • Paint, chemicals, and solvents
  • Medical waste and expired medicines
  • Motor oil and automotive fluids

Warning: Never mix hazardous waste with regular trash. Contact LGU for proper disposal!

Best Practices for Waste Segregation

Use Separate Bins

Have clearly labeled bins for each waste category at home.

Clean Recyclables

Rinse containers before putting them in the recyclable bin.

Reduce First

The best waste is the waste that's never created. Reduce consumption.

Educate Family

Make sure everyone in your household knows how to segregate properly.
